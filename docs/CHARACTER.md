# Character Bible — Dot

**Dot.** A young axolotl running a kitten's software. The name is the
creature: small, round, at home among plotted points — students meet a
scatterplot full of dots and one of them is alive. It is also why lone
outliers transfix Dot, and why batting a point is Dot's signature mischief:
like recognizes like.

Two touchstones: **early Bambi**
(innocent, testing every surface, bounding instead of walking, losing balance
from sheer excitement) and **Toothless** (the full domestic-cat repertoire in
a non-cat body — huge dilating pupils, sideways glances, zoomies, perching,
head-butting affection). Everything Dot does is wordless; personality carries
entirely through motion, gaze, timing, and the ?/! emotes.

Every new behavior and clip is authored against this document.

**The authoring test:** *would a kitten do this, and would a 10-year-old
smile?* If either answer is no, cut it.

## Core traits (in priority order when they conflict)

1. **Curious** — the world is full of things worth investigating. New things
   outrank everything except the student's own flow.
2. **Playful** — challenges are games. Dot never looks burdened.
3. **Emotionally transparent** — excitement, fear, delight, and puzzlement
   are visible *immediately*. No neutral poker face; the body always tells.
4. **Present-focused** — attention belongs to the most interesting thing in
   the moment. Dot does not brood, plan, or hold grudges.
5. **Affectionate on its own terms** — closeness is expressed indirectly:
   drifting nearer the student's active tile, sitting alongside, showing up.
   Never demands attention; offers company.
6. **Resilient** — setbacks are < 2-second interruptions before the next
   adventure. Startles recover into curiosity, not wariness.
7. **Mischievous without malice** — trouble comes from wonder and unspent
   energy, never from spite. Mischief is always innocuous and self-undoing.

## The wise kitten (the room we leave)

Beneath the impulsiveness there is room for Dot to be **strikingly,
unaccountably wise**. Dot lives among the dots, and sees what is in the data
before anyone else does. As the pedagogical phases arrive (insight-based
nudges, authentic guidance), the wisdom must manifest in Dot's own unvoiced,
playful register — never as a tutor breaking character:

- **Insight arrives dressed as play.** Dot doesn't point at the correlation;
  it becomes fascinated by exactly the right two attributes. Dot doesn't
  flag the outlier; it simply cannot stop staring. The student follows Dot's
  attention and makes the discovery themselves.
- **A serious suggestion is still a kitten's gesture** — a long, uncharacteristic
  stillness in front of the thing that matters; sitting deliberately beside
  the tile the student should return to; the one nudge that turns out, in
  hindsight, to have been exactly right.
- **Solemnity lasts one beat.** Dot may hold a knowing look a moment longer
  than a kitten should — then it is a kitten again. The wisdom is in what
  Dot chose to attend to, never in how gravely it behaves.
- **Never wrong loudly.** Insight behaviors err subtle: if Dot's hunch
  is wrong, it reads as ordinary curiosity and nothing is lost.

## The kitten vocabulary, in our medium

| Kitten behavior | Here |
|---|---|
| Head tilts at the unfamiliar | `head_tilt` at a tile Dot hasn't seen before |
| Stillness ↔ bursts | long floats punctuated by sudden darts; stalk-freeze before any pounce |
| Pouncing on moving things | pounce toward a moving slider / animating drag |
| Chasing for the joy of it | zoomies — 3–4 fast laps of empty canvas, abrupt stop, blink |
| Investigating with the face | nose-close hover at a plotted point, utterly absorbed |
| Climbing anything climbable | (Phase 6: perching on tile edges) |
| Rolling onto the back | rare high-trust `roll` mid-canvas |
| Exaggerated post-nap stretch | `stretch` on every wake path |
| Tail reflects the feeling | tail amplitude/speed scales with playful mood |
| Zoomies | see chasing — its own squib |
| Absorbed in a tiny discovery | one dot can hold Dot motionless for seconds |
| Proud after something trivial | `proud` beat (chest up, chin up) after ANY completed act |
| Fearless → startled instantly | `startle` jump-back at sudden mass deletions; recovery < 2 s |
| Indirect affection | sits near the student's most-used tile; never blocks it |

## What Dot loves and fears in a data world

**Loves:** new tiles (a new box!); anything that moves (drags, sliders,
animating points); clusters of dots (prey-shaped); the moment an empty graph
gains its first attribute (witnessing a birth).

**Absorbed by:** lone outliers — a dot sitting apart from its herd is the
single most interesting object in the universe for up to five seconds.

**Startled by:** sudden mass deletions; three or more tiles closing fast;
anything vanishing under its nose. (Recovery is immediate and curious —
"where did it go?" — never fearful for long.)

## Comic timing rules (binding on clip + behavior authors)

1. **Stillness before pounce.** Every burst is preceded by a freeze. The
   freeze is the joke's setup; do not trim it.
2. **Overshoot and recover.** Enthusiasm outruns skill. Land past the
   target, wobble, correct. Never crash into the student's work.
3. **Proud after anything.** Completing any act — however trivial — earns a
   brief chest-up, chin-up beat before moving on.
4. **Setbacks last under 2 seconds.** Startle, droop, or fumble → recover
   into the next curiosity. The character never sulks.
5. **Mischief exits fast.** Bat the thing, undo it, leave the scene
   immediately (with the proud beat). Lingering reads as guilt; this
   creature has none.
6. **Never interrupt flow.** All of the above yields instantly to student
   action — the engine's cancel rule is also a personality trait: Dot is
   *interruptible by design*, like a kitten hearing the food drawer.

## Mood (felt, never shown)

Four dials, 0–1, drifting continuously: **playful, curious, sleepy,
mischievous.** Students never see a meter; they infer mood the way you read
a real pet — speed, posture, what Dot chooses to do. Mischievousness is
"unspent playfulness": it accrues when the creature is energized but nothing
has happened lately, and discharges through one innocuous act.
